The Mystery of Dark Matter

Scientists have long been puzzled by the fact that the universe appears to contain far more matter than we can see. This invisible material, known as dark matter, doesn’t emit or reflect light but exerts a powerful gravitational pull on galaxies...

The search for dark matter has led researchers deep underground and into space in hopes of detecting rare interactions with ordinary matter. Although we haven’t directly observed it, dark matter makes up around 27% of the universe. Understanding it could completely reshape physics and our understanding of the cosmos.
